Why It Matters

Why language matters so much

Every conversation, song and story you share with your child is building their brain. Here is why the early years are so important.

🧠

Builds thinking and reasoning

Language helps children organise their thoughts, make sense of the world, and solve problems. The richer their language, the deeper their thinking (AGDE, 2022).

🤝

Builds friendships and relationships

Children who communicate well find it easier to connect with others, express their feelings, and navigate social situations (Education Review Office, 2023).

📚

Prepares children for school

Strong oral language before school is one of the strongest predictors of reading and writing success. Children learn to read by building on language they already have (Johnston, 2010).

🌟

Shapes identity and belonging

When children's language — in all its forms — is valued and celebrated, their sense of self grows stronger. Every language is a strength (AGDE, 2022).

Developmental Stages

Growing through language

Every child develops at their own pace. Here are the key stages — from babbling to storytelling.

Birth – 18 months
Early Communicators
  • Cooing and babbling
  • Responding to voices
  • Loving songs and rhymes
  • Interest in picture books
12 – 36 months
Early Language Users
  • First words appearing
  • Vocabulary growing fast
  • Combining two words
  • Interest in rhymes
30 – 60 months
Emergent Literacy Learners
  • Speaking in sentences
  • Telling simple stories
  • Recognising rhymes
  • Clapping syllables
5+ years
School Ready Communicators
  • Complex sentences
  • Detailed storytelling
  • Real conversations
  • Beginning to read

Home Tips

6 things you can do at home today

You do not need expensive resources or special programs. Your voice, your time and your stories are the most powerful tools available.

Tip 01

📖 Read together every day

Even 10 minutes builds vocabulary, listening skills and a love of stories. Talk about the pictures — you do not have to read every word.

Tip 02

🎵 Sing songs and rhymes

Nursery rhymes build the sound skills children need to learn to read. You do not need to be a great singer!

Tip 03

💬 Have real conversations

Back-and-forth talk is more powerful for brain development than simply hearing lots of words (Romeo et al., 2018).

Tip 04

🎭 Encourage pretend play

Join their play and ask "What happens next?" Pretend play builds vocabulary, grammar and imagination.

Tip 05

📚 Create a reading corner

Books at child height send a powerful message. Visit the library together and let them choose books.

Tip 06

🌍 Use your home language

A strong home language makes learning English easier. Bilingualism is a superpower (VCAA, 2020).
